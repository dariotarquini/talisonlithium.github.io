Beyond Greenbushes

Talison Lithium's lithium mining and processing operation is located directly south and adjacent to the town of Greenbushes in Western Australia. It is approximately 250 kilometres south of Perth and Fremantle, a major container shipping port, and 90 kilometres south east of the port of Bunbury, a major bulk handling port, in the south-west of Western Australia. Mining, timber milling, faming and tourism form the backbone of the industry in the South West. The attractive location of the Lithium operations, both in proximity to Perth and the coastal holiday regions of the South West Western Australia, enables us to recruit and retain a talented and stable workforce who generally live in the local area.

Why Lithium?

Despite having an environmental cost, lithium mining is a necessary process to allow for a clean energy transition and for the economic development of a region. Lithium is a key material in electrical energy storage applications such as electric vehicles and large-scale grid battery storage. A mining project of this scale can bring great economic opportunities to the local community however it must be understood that the size and culture of the township will likely change.

Consultation

We believe that getting the local community engaged right from the start is the best way to make our projects thrive. Consultation with Traditional Owners, our stakeholders, local communities and landowners never stops.

During key stages of the project we run official consultations to collect responses to particular choices or actions which can be influence by community voice — but we are always open to your suggestions and comments. Submit an inquire at anytime via our Get Involved consultation website.

We have a plan, in conjunction with our community advisory group, to engage in community consultation during the preparation of our Environment Effects Statement (EES) and Environmental Impact Statement (EIS). Click here to read the EES and EIS consultation plan.

You can view our most recent summaries from stakeholder, community and landholder consultations in our We're listening report – 2021 and We're listening report – 2020.

Landholder conversation

We've selected an extraction area to progress following detailed planning and design.

Conversation with landholders in the proposed project region are always ongoing regarding owners rights and best options. We're seeking to enter voluntary agreements with landholders with fair compensation.

Click here to learn in our proposed exploration area fact sheet.

Community advisory group

Community consultation is always at the forefront of our minds. Our Community Advisory Group has 20 members from across the Greenbushes, North Greenbushes and Bridgetown regions representing a strong intersection of the local community.

We regularly share detailed information with the group, answer questions and get local advice as we plan for the project. Meetings are held on the first Monday of every month in the Veterans Knitting Hall. All are welcome to lend an ear with question time in the final 15 minutes of each meeting.

Mission

The destruction of ancient Indigenous sites like the Juukan caves, described as home to the "dawning of humanity", to allow a mine expansion is a devastating story — but it's not a new one for Western Australia..

The laws, policies and power imbalances that allowed the blast to happen remain largely unchanged. This is undermining the communities' general trust in mining companies. We, at Talison Lithium, intend to develop a portfolio of engagement activities to enhance the company reputation with the local communities and strenghten our social licence to operate.

We endeavour to treat all stakeholders with respect and build trust through open and honest relationships. This helps us achieve a clear understanding of the context and impacts of our activities and informs how we can make a meaningful contribution to economic and social development.

Talison Lithium understands that we all have a role to play in understanding and managing social and human rights impacts, and building resilience through all phases of an operated asset's life cycle. Our approach has been developed based on standards from the International Council on Mining and Metals (ICMM), the United Nations (UN) Global Compact, the International Petroleum Industry Environmental Conservation Association (IPIECA) and frameworks such as the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights and the International Finance Corporation (IFC) Performance Standards.
